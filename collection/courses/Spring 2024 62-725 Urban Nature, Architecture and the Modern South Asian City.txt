<start course description for 62-725 Urban Nature, Architecture and the Modern South Asian City>
Semester: Spring 2024 (aka S24)
Course Description: Representations of cities in the so-called global-south are often underpinned by a discourse of lagging modernity amidst unrelenting urbanization, fractured infrastructures, social inequities, and climate catastrophe: Broken pipes, polluted atmospheres, tangled wires, waste dumps, and dense informal settlements set against an aspiring skyline. In this seminar, we critically probe such presentations and examine the political ecologies that contour these forms of urbanization through theoretically informed and historically grounded examinations of South Asia cities from the mid-19th century onwards. As a critical nexus of the effects of the global climate crisis, South Asia provides a unique and urgent case study to interrogate not just the technical but also cultural, social, political, and material dimensions of urban nature and the nature of the urban.
