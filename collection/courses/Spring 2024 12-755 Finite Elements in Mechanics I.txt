<start course description for 12-755 Finite Elements in Mechanics I>
Semester: Spring 2024 (aka S24)
Course Description: The basic theory and applications of the finite element method in mechanics are presented. Development of the FEM as a Galerkin method for numerical solution of boundary value problems. Applications to second-order steady problems, including heat conduction, elasticity, convective transport, viscous flow, and others. Introduction to advanced topics, including fourth-order equations, time dependence, and nonlinear problems.Prerequisite: Graduate standing or consent of instructor.
