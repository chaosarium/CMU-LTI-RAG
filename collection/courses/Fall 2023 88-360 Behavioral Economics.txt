<start course description for 88-360 Behavioral Economics>
Semester: Fall 2023 (aka F23)
Course Description: This course introduces students to behavioral economics, an emerging  subfield of economics that incorporates insights from psychology and other social sciences into economics. We will examine evidence on how human  behavior systematically departs from the standard assumptions of economics, and then investigate attempts by behavioral economists to improve economic analyses.
