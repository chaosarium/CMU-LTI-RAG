<start course description for 82-425 Contemporary German Film Comedies>
Semester: Fall 2023 (aka F23)
Course Description: This repeatable course explores the culture of the German-speaking nations through a thematic or conceptual focus. Students critically analyze authentic documents, for example, historical, biographical, and literary texts, as well as film and works of the visual arts while improving and expanding their language skills.  Fall 2023: Are the Germans funny? Do they have a sense of humor? The answer is yes! U.S. stereotypes of the Germans suggest that they are always serious and negative and never funny. Nothing could be further from the truth! Comedy is actually by far the most popular German film genre, easily surpassing other genres (including historical films and dramas). The Germans love humor. But German humor is very different from humor in the U.S., and it doesn't "travel" across the Atlantic particularly well. This course, conducted entirely in German, will explore at least fourteen very popular post-1989 German film comedies, including Go Trabi Go (1991) and (T)Raumschiff SurprisePeriode 1 (2004), a send-up of the popular U.S. Star Trek series. The course will demonstrate that German film comedy is no joke but rather big business! Students will be required to lead one class discussion (in German), to write brief reviews of the relevant films, and to complete a final project, which may take the form of a short paper or a film comedy (in German).
