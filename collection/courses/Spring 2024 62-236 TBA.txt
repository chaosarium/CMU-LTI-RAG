Semester: Spring 2024 (aka S24)
Course Description: In Kiln 1 students will gain a broad understanding of the vast possibilities of kilnformed glass exploring both fusing and casting techniques. Students will learn how to cut sheet glass and work with a variety of other glass materials to melt and shape their designs creating an array of functional and decorative pieces.  Using a found object students will learn how to reproduce the object as in glass. Students will gain an understanding of kiln programming and the properties of glass. No experience necessary.  Registration can only be done on or after your scheduled registration day. Please email Stefanie McGowan (stefanim@andrew.cmu.edu) for more information and to register. Registration is first come, first served. Course fee is $240. Class is held at Pittsburgh Glass Center. Courses at the Pittsburgh Glass Center will be held from February 6th- April 7th.
