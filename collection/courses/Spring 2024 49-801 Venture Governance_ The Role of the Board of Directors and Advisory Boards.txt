<start course description for 49-801 Venture Governance: The Role of the Board of Directors and Advisory Boards>
Semester: Spring 2024 (aka S24)
Course Description: The goal of the course is to teach founders, CEOs, investors and key employees of a high-tech startup venture the requirements and process to be a CEO or Director and how to manage and communicate with stakeholders e.g. Investors, Board of Directors, Shareholders, Advisory Boards and key employees. The CEO office and boardroom is where the governance and funding of the venture occurs.   The course will cover 3 stages of a start-up growth: Seed, Scale, and Exit with emphasis on the Seed and Scale stages. It explores frameworks for understanding the complex dynamics among CEOs, Board, executives, investors, shareholders and the unique requirements as the firm scales.  Note: Priority registration will be given to the in-person A4 section. The R4 section of this course is specifically reserved for students in the Part-Time Remote MSSM program. Students on the R4 waitlist will be admitted depending on availability.
</end course description for 49-801 Venture Governance: The Role of the Board of Directors and Advisory Boards>
